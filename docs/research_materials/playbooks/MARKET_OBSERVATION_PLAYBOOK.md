
# Market Observation Playbook

Last updated: 2026-03-08

This playbook converts recurring market observations into hypothesis and strategy actions.

## 1) AI / Mega-cap leadership is dominant
- Meaning: Leadership is concentrated; breadth may lag index-level strength.
- Suggested hypotheses: momentum persistence, sector leadership continuation, trend exhaustion watch.
- Strategy families: Momentum/Trend, Relative Strength/Rotation.
- Regime implications: trend or uptrend_normal; monitor concentration risk and late-cycle exhaustion.

## 2) Volatility has compressed sharply
- Meaning: Range contraction can precede directional expansion.
- Suggested hypotheses: compression breakout, false-break trap, transition ignition.
- Strategy families: Volatility Compression/Expansion, Regime Transition.
- Regime implications: range_normal transitioning toward trend/high_volatility.

## 3) Funding rates are extreme (crypto)
- Meaning: Crowded derivatives positioning; elevated squeeze or unwind probability.
- Suggested hypotheses: funding dislocation reversion, carry unwind, basis normalization.
- Strategy families: Crypto Funding/Basis/Carry.
- Regime implications: range_high_vol or risk-off transition risk depending on OI behavior.

## 4) Small caps outperforming large caps
- Meaning: Risk appetite broadening; potential style rotation.
- Suggested hypotheses: rotation persistence, laggard catch-up, breadth-backed continuation.
- Strategy families: Relative Strength/Rotation, Momentum/Trend.
- Regime implications: risk_recovery or early trend re-acceleration.

## 5) Macro proxies show risk-off behavior
- Meaning: Cross-asset stress; prioritize capital preservation and selective execution.
- Suggested hypotheses: risk-on to risk-off transition, panic behavior, liquidity stress.
- Strategy families: Regime Transition, Liquidity/Stress, defensive Mean Reversion only.
- Regime implications: risk_off or stress_risk_off; reduce sizing and increase no-trade frequency.

## 6) Credit spreads widening while equities hold up
- Meaning: Hidden fragility; surface trend may not reflect internal risk deterioration.
- Suggested hypotheses: failed breakout risk, transition warning, defensive rotation.
- Strategy families: Regime Transition, False Breakout/Fade, Relative Strength (defensive tilt).
- Regime implications: transition from trend to range/high-vol possible.

## 7) Crypto open interest surges without price progress
- Meaning: Position crowding and liquidation risk increase.
- Suggested hypotheses: squeeze setup, failed trend capture, funding/basis stress.
- Strategy families: Crypto-Native, False Breakout/Failed Move.
- Regime implications: high_volatility with higher whipsaw probability.

## 8) Repeated failed breakouts in index proxies
- Meaning: Trend quality deteriorating; trap dynamics dominate.
- Suggested hypotheses: false breakout fade, range reversion, transition degradation.
- Strategy families: Mean Reversion, Regime Transition.
- Regime implications: range or range_high_vol; suppress aggressive trend templates.

## 9) Liquidity deteriorates (wider spreads, thinner depth)
- Meaning: Execution risk rises and backtest assumptions become fragile.
- Suggested hypotheses: liquidity shock reversal, stress filters, no-trade emphasis.
- Strategy families: Liquidity/Stress/Exhaustion, defensive transition templates.
- Regime implications: high_volatility/stress state; strict size caps.

## 10) Event week (FOMC/CPI/NFP/earnings concentration)
- Meaning: Regime may shift intraday; false moves and spread jumps increase.
- Suggested hypotheses: event shock filter, post-event drift, vol-decay transition.
- Strategy families: Regime Transition, Volatility templates, selective Momentum.
- Regime implications: temporary high_volatility; maintain adaptive thresholds and conservative execution.

## Practical Usage Rules
- Convert each observation into explicit hypothesis candidates, not generic commentary.
- Update discovery constraints weekly based on observed regime and execution quality.
- Record when an observation changed posture from GO to REDUCE/SKIP.
